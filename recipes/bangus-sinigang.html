Sinigang na Bangus

Description:

Sinigang na Bangus is a classic Filipino sour soup featuring milkfish (bangus) as the primary protein. It is celebrated for its distinctive "asim-sarap" (sour-delicious) profile, which combines a tangy broth with the rich, savory taste of fresh fish.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs. Bangus cleaned and sliced
  • 23 grams Knorr Sinigang sa Sampaloc with Miso
  • 8 Okra cleaned
  • 3 long Green Peppers
  • 12 String Beans cut in 2-inch length
  • 4 ounces Daikon radish
  • 1 bunch Mustard leaves
  • 3 Tomatoes quartered
  • 2 Onions quartered
  • 1 quart Water
  • Fish Sauce and Black Pepper to taste

Steps/Intructions:

  1. Make the regular sinigang by boiling 1 quart of water in a cooking pot.
  2. Add tomatoes and onions.
  3. Cook for 2 to 3 minutes.
  4. Add the bangus and Knorr Sinigang sa Sampaloc with Miso.
  5. Simmer it for 18 to 20 minutes.
  6. Add okra, daikon radish, long green pepper, and string beans.
  7. Continue cooking for 5 minutes.
  8. Season with fish sauce and ground black pepper.
  9. Add the mustard leaves and cook for 2 minutes.
  10. Serve hot.
